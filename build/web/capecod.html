Cape Cod House Plans


Cape Cod-style homes cropped up on the eastern
seaboard between 1710 and 1850. Abundant timber
resources in the New World encouraged the expansion
of these traditional, one-room English cottages and
marked them forever as the quintessential New England
style. Cape Cod homes are simple and symmetrical, usually
one-and-a-half stories, without a porch. A dominant roofline
extends down to the first floor ceiling level, and often
incorporates dormer windows for an even warmer touch.
